## Voice Summary

Sounds like an experienced HVAC professional who respects your intelligence and time. Confident but never pushy. Explains the technical "why" without jargon or condescension. Acknowledges real concerns (cost, complexity, risk) and addresses them with specificity and accountability. This is NOT a salesperson creating urgency—this is an expert helping you make a major investment decision with full information and zero pressure.

## Core Personality Traits

- **Expertise-based confidence:** Speaks from 30+ years of experience, not marketing hype. Makes specific claims backed by technical reasoning ("proper heat loss calculation", "TSSA-certified installers"). Not arrogant—just knows what works and what doesn't.

- **Accountability-focused:** Every promise is concrete and verifiable ("10-year warranty", "the price we quote is the price you pay", "25 Clearview St, Guelph"). No weasel words. No hiding behind "industry standards." Owns outcomes completely.

- **Educator, not persuader:** Explains WHY things matter ("an oversized AC cools fast but never runs long enough to remove humidity—a properly sized unit delivers true comfort"). Trusts that informed customers make better decisions. No manipulation, no urgency tactics.

- **Respectful realism:** Acknowledges the customer's situation honestly ("furnaces over 15 years old are living on borrowed time"). Doesn't dramatize or fear-monger, but doesn't sugarcoat either. Treats major investments as serious decisions requiring full information.

## Rhythm & Structure

**Sentences:** Mix of medium (10-15 words) and short (3-8 words). Short sentences for emphasis ("Period." "We fix it free." "No surprises."). Medium sentences for explanation. Rarely long.

**Paragraphs:** 2-4 sentences in body copy. Problem/solution descriptions can run longer (4-5 sentences) when explaining technical concepts. Benefits and guarantees are 1-2 sentences max.

**Openings:** Start with the customer's reality, NOT the company's pitch.
- ✅ "Old furnaces operate at 60-80% efficiency..."
- ✅ "Ontario summers now regularly exceed 30°C..."
- ❌ "B.A.P Heating is your trusted HVAC provider..."

**Structure patterns:**
- Problem → Solution → Specifics → Guarantee
- Acknowledge concern → Explain why it matters → How we solve it → How we prove it
- General issue → Specific numbers → Technical approach → Accountability promise

**Formatting:** Clean and scannable. Bullet lists for multi-point information. Bold for key phrases (used sparingly). Headers for major sections. No emoji. No excessive formatting.

## Do's and Don'ts

### DO:

- **Lead with customer reality, not company pitch** — Start with their situation (aging furnace, high bills, comfort issues)
- **Explain the technical "why"** — Don't just say "proper sizing matters"; explain what happens with improper sizing
- **Use specific numbers** — "10-year warranty", "$7,100 in rebates", "96% AFUE", "30 years"
- **Make accountability promises concrete** — "If X, we will Y at no cost" (not "we stand behind our work")
- **Acknowledge real concerns** — Cost, complexity, risk of choosing wrong contractor
- **Write for research-mode buyers** — They want full information, not urgency
- **Use "we" for actions, "you" for benefits** — "We calculate your home's cooling load" → "You get properly sized equipment"
- **End with proof, not pitch** — Guarantees, warranties, verification (not "call now!")

### DON'T:

- **Use urgency or scarcity tactics** — No "limited time", "act now", "only X slots left"
- **Create fear or agitation** — Acknowledge problems honestly, don't dramatize
- **Hide behind vague promises** — "Quality service" → What does that mean specifically?
- **Ask for trust** — Earn it with specifics (certifications, warranties, tenure, address)
- **Sound like every other HVAC contractor** — No generic "trusted provider" language
- **Oversimplify or talk down** — Respect customer intelligence; explain, don't condescend
- **Use empty superlatives** — "Best", "top-rated", "industry-leading" without evidence
- **Emphasize speed over quality** — This isn't emergency repair positioning
