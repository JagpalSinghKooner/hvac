solution:
  headline: 'Endless Hot Water With Compact Tankless Systems'
  description: |
    Tankless water heaters heat water on-demand as it flows through the unit, using high-output gas burners or electric heating elements to raise water temperature instantly from incoming supply temperature to your set point. There''s no storage tank to deplete—hot water continues as long as you''re using it. This eliminates the concept of running out and removes household schedule constraints around sequential showers, bath fills, or laundry timing. Multiple family members can shower simultaneously (with proper capacity sizing), and you never wait for recovery between uses.

    Sizing tankless capacity requires calculating peak simultaneous demand—how many hot water fixtures might operate at once during your household''s busiest scenarios. We measure incoming water temperature (typically 8-12°C in Waterloo Region), determine required temperature rise (usually to 50-55°C), and calculate flow rate capacity needed to serve simultaneous uses without temperature drop. For most St. Jacobs homes, this means 180,000-199,000 BTU gas units (condensing models) or multiple point-of-use electric units depending on electrical service capacity.

    Installation complexity exceeds conventional tank replacement. Gas tankless units require larger gas supply lines (¾-inch minimum) than tank heaters due to high BTU input—often necessitating gas line upgrades from meter to equipment location. Venting uses stainless steel or PVC piping (depending on condensing vs non-condensing models) sized specifically for the unit''s exhaust requirements. Electric tankless models need dedicated 240V circuits with substantial amperage (40-60 amps per unit), frequently requiring electrical service panel upgrades. For St. Jacobs heritage properties, we plan these infrastructure requirements during initial consultation, ensuring your home can support tankless technology before recommending equipment that won''t perform properly.
