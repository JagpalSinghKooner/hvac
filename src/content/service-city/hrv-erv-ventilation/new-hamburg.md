  headline: 'HRV and ERV Systems for Healthy Fresh Air'
  description: |
    Our ventilation system design begins with calculating home volume and occupancy determining required fresh air cfm meeting ASHRAE 62.2 residential ventilation standards. Ontario Building Code now mandates mechanical ventilation in new construction—properly sized systems deliver 50-100 cfm continuous fresh air depending on home size and occupancy. We evaluate existing ductwork determining whether HRV/ERV systems integrate with forced-air distribution or require dedicated ducting. Many New Hamburg homes benefit from systems delivering fresh air to bedrooms and living areas while exhausting stale air from bathrooms and laundry rooms.

    HRV (Heat Recovery Ventilator) systems suit most New Hamburg homes operating forced-air furnaces with AC. Heat exchange cores transfer 70-80% of exhaust air temperature to incoming fresh air preventing extreme temperature losses. Winter operation preheats incoming -20°C air using outgoing 21°C household air, delivering 10-15°C supply air to furnaces rather than freezing outdoor air. This recovered heat reduces furnace runtime 12-18% compared to natural ventilation approaches. Summer operation pre-cools incoming hot air using air-conditioned exhaust streams reducing AC loads.

    ERV (Energy Recovery Ventilator) systems add moisture transfer to heat exchange—summer operation prevents humid outdoor air from adding latent cooling loads, while winter operation retains household humidity preventing excessive dryness. ERV technology suits homes without whole-home humidifiers or properties experiencing basement moisture management challenges. The energy penalty transferring both sensible and latent heat costs 5-8% more than HRV equipment but provides superior humidity control protecting home finishes and occupant comfort.
