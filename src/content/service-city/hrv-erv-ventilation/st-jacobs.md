solution:
  headline: 'ERV and HRV Systems That Improve Indoor Air'
  description: 'Heat Recovery Ventilators (HRVs) and Energy Recovery Ventilators (ERVs) provide controlled fresh air exchange while recovering heating energy that would otherwise be wasted. Both systems exhaust stale indoor air and bring in fresh outdoor air continuously, passing both streams through a heat exchanger core that transfers energy between them. In winter, outgoing warm air preheats incoming cold air before it enters your home—recovering 60-90% of heating energy depending on system efficiency. In summer, the process reverses, precooling incoming warm air with outgoing cool air. ERVs add moisture transfer capability—recovering some humidity in winter (reducing static electricity and heating system moisture loss) and rejecting humidity in summer (reducing cooling load). For St. Jacobs heritage homes, we integrate ventilation systems with existing forced-air distribution when ductwork exists, or install standalone systems with dedicated ductwork for properties without central HVAC. Installation considers building layout, occupancy patterns, and existing mechanical systems. Proper fresh air distribution prevents stagnant zones, and balanced intake/exhaust prevents pressure imbalances that cause drafts or backdrafting of combustion appliances. Systems include automatic controls that adjust ventilation based on indoor humidity or CO2 levels—ensuring adequate fresh air without over-ventilating and wasting energy.'
