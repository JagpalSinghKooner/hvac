solution:
  headline: 'Modern Boiler Systems for Consistent Radiant Heat'
  description: |
    Our boiler installation process begins with comprehensive heat loss calculation using Manual J methodology—we measure building envelope characteristics, window areas and orientations, insulation levels, and infiltration rates to determine actual heating loads. Radiant floor heat and baseboard radiation require different water temperatures and flow rates, affecting boiler selection and circulator sizing. Load calculations account for these factors, ensuring equipment capacity matches demand without the oversizing that causes short-cycling or undersizing that creates comfort problems during extreme cold.

    Equipment selection balances efficiency ratings with installation requirements and long-term operating costs. Condensing boilers achieve 95%+ AFUE efficiency but require condensate drainage, acidic flue venting, and sealed combustion air intake. Non-condensing equipment achieves 85-89% efficiency with simpler venting using existing chimneys in many applications, reducing installation complexity and cost. We explain trade-offs clearly—higher efficiency equipment delivers greater operating savings but involves additional installation requirements that affect total project cost and complexity.

    Venting design follows manufacturer specifications and TSSA code requirements. Condensing boilers require PVC or stainless steel flue materials handling acidic condensate, with proper slope for drainage and termination locations meeting clearance requirements for building openings, property lines, and mechanical air intakes. We coordinate penetrations through building envelope with attention to finished surfaces and weatherproofing. Sealed combustion air intakes prevent indoor air usage, improving comfort and safety while meeting code requirements for mechanical room ventilation.
