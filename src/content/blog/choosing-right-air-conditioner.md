Choosing the right air conditioner for your home is an important decision that affects your comfort, energy bills, and indoor air quality for years to come. Here's what you need to know.

## Sizing Matters

The most critical factor is proper sizing. An oversized unit cycles on and off frequently, wasting energy and failing to adequately dehumidify. An undersized unit runs constantly and struggles to cool your home. Our team performs detailed load calculations to determine the perfect size for your space.

## Energy Efficiency Ratings

Look for systems with high SEER (Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio) ratings. While higher-efficiency units cost more upfront, they save significantly on energy bills over time. Modern systems can have SEER ratings of 16 or higher.

## System Types

Consider which type of system best suits your needs:

- **Central Air:** Ideal for whole-home cooling with existing ductwork
- **Heat Pumps:** Provide both heating and cooling efficiently
- **Ductless Mini-Splits:** Perfect for homes without ductwork or room additions

## Additional Features

Modern air conditioners offer features like variable-speed compressors, smart thermostats, and advanced air filtration. These enhancements improve comfort and air quality while reducing energy consumption.
